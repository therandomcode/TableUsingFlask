Is the data complete?

Most of the data is very complete, with the exception of the 'major instruments' column. I doesn't damage the data for me, as I'm not terribly sure what exactly is meant by 'major instruments' anyway. I assumed the major component of any observatory would be a telescope... On a serious note, I would also expect to see some data concerning the range of the telescope, the number of days it's actually useful per year (based on clear weather) and possibly an upgrade/maintenance history.

Is the data coherent?

There were definitely some problems with this dataset. For example, the Asian Telescope is listed at an elevation of 100'000'000 km. For reference, the diameter of Earth is only 12'742 km. (According to it's Wikipedia page, it's at only 700 m.)

Does it make sense relative to itself? Does it match your expectations? Are the distributions sensible? What is your evidence for this?

Is the data correct?

In order to verify correctness, I decided to do search Google and spot check three of the telescopes on my list (in addition to the Asian Telescope, which I'd already looked out.)
